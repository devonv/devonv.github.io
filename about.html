Devon V. Johnson

I'm just a college student interested in folklore and art.

(I also really like plants.)

I will graduate with honors from Virginia Tech in December 2014 with a BA in English and minors in horticulture and Applachian studies. I'm a member of Phi Beta Kappa and, by the time I graduate, I will have completed my undergraduate honors thesis (and associated thesis project) on quilt documentation efforts in Virginia's New River valley.

I am primarily interested in understanding the way that humans interact with objects--particularly vernacular "folk" art objects. I'm interested in commodification and material culture. I like the Frankfurt School and bell hooks. I am entirely, utterly devoted to NPR, with a complete commitment to This American Life. I also like to watch PBS with my Nana and to knit. I think all of these things are related.

In the immediate future, I would like to pursue graduate study in folklore or material culture. Perhaps one day I'd like to get my PhD... Maybe. For now, I know that I am interested continuing my work as a crafts person (and writing magnet poems) and in trying to understand our complicated relationship with material objects.
