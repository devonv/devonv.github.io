Technical Documents

- Women's Center Manaul -

This is a receptionist manual I created during my internship with the Virginia Tech Women's Center. The original manual was a disorganized binder with papers and fact-sheets by many different authors, with no standard style. Part of my responsibility as an intern was to compile these sheets into a coherent manual with an index. I had to organize and rewrite most manual content.

I was also responsible for the formatting choices. I chose a system of check-mark bullets and a clear page number system. Color and font choices were based on the Virginia Tech Women's Center style guide, and the finished binder is stylistically coherent with other Women's Center publications.

- SOP Proposal -

This is a proposal for a standard operating procedure project. Proposals are an important part of the technical writing and product development process. This proposal was for creation of a manual binder for the webmaster position in my community service organization. When I took over the position, there was no documentation or guiding document. The binder proposed in this document would include necessary information for new webmasters.

This proposal is formatted according to conventional formatting stanadards, however I chose font styes myself. Sections in this propsal fit assignment criteria and address important aspects of proposal writing, like timeline and audience.

This is a paper I wrote for a professional writing class called "Science Writing." This essay is meant to mimic popular writing on scientific studies. I chose the topic of GMO corn in Mexico because I was interested in writing about an issue with scientific and cultural significance. Corn in Mexico is a topic that transcends pure scientific discussion.

This is a good example of a writing piece meant for a popular audience. The promt for this paper was to create a piece of science writing that would appeal to a wide audience.
